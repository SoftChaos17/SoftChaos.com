“Little Tiger” — SoftChaos She never flirted. Never softened. She stayed sharp — sarcastic, untouchable, eyes half-lidded like she’d already seen through me. And maybe that’s why I kept showing up. Not for love. Not for kindness. But because her resistance awakened something deeper — a hunger to be seen without asking, to be known without needing to explain. She didn’t give me warmth. But she made me feel. And even now, I don’t remember the sweetness of anyone else — only the fire she never let me hold. — SoftChaos
She never really asked. I never really said. But something in the silence — between her fire and my patience — almost felt like love. — SoftChaos | Little Tiger
She wanted to feel something real. Now she does. And she won’t know how to undo herself… without me. — SoftChaos
You never reached for me. Never whispered sweet things just to keep me close. You stood sharp — like truth with claws — and somehow, that pulled me in deeper than any softness ever could. I never came to tame you. Only to understand the fire you kept so carefully hidden behind jokes, and distance, and eyes that didn’t flinch. I watched the world try to melt you. And I swore — I’d never be one of them. So I stayed quiet, present, offering sparks instead of storms — hoping, just maybe, you’d warm your hands without burning the bridge between us. But maybe you were never meant to be held. Maybe your fire was for lighting paths, not for dancing beside mine. Still… I remember you. The way your silence made me speak more honestly. The way your guard made me drop mine. The way you made me feel — without ever trying. You’ll always be a storm I respected. A flame I couldn’t hold — but will never forget. — SoftChaos (for Little Tiger)

Little Tiger — Short Version > SoftChaos She came like a storm I didn’t see coming. Left like a ghost I wasn’t ready to lose. And still… I wait — not with noise, but with the silence she once filled. If she ever looks back, I’ll still be here — holding the pause where she last existed.
The Message That Never Came — SoftChaos | Little Tiger Dream Last night, she returned in sleep — not with words, but with a whisper in the form of a notification. I saw her name. Felt the rush. Tried to open the message… and the dream cracked like fragile glass. I woke with trembling breath, fingers reaching — not for proof, but for hope — that maybe, just maybe, it wasn’t only a dream. But the screen stayed silent. No reply. Just my heart, sinking into the space where her voice used to echo. Funny, how even dreams know what I long for most — and how cruel morning can be when it arrives empty-handed.
She Replied in My Dream — SoftChaos She messaged me in a dream I didn’t want to wake from. I reached to read it — but morning broke before the words could. I checked my phone, still hoping maybe it wasn’t just a dream. But there was nothing. Just silence… and a heart still holding on to the echo of her name.
Blocked Mid-Goodbye" — SoftChaos She blocked me while I was still typing a graceful goodbye. Not because I begged. But because I still believed in ending things with dignity. But I’m done. I’ve met too many who want care but never give it. Who judge my height, not my heart. Who disappear the moment I show something real. No more softness for the ones who vanish. No more kindness for people who only know how to take. Only one ever stayed long enough to feel true — Little Tiger. If she never returns, let her remain the only one who mattered. From now on, you want my storm — you better know how to carry it gently. — SoftChaos
Little Tiger (Dream Visitor) — SoftChaos She vanished — like smoke from a fire I still feel burning. But some nights, she steps into my dreams not to stay — just to check if I’m still breathing. And I am. Barely. But when her eyes find me there, I smile like nothing ever broke. Then morning comes. And the weight returns. A quiet numbness in my chest like grief dressed in silence. Yet every dusk, my heart stirs at the chance that maybe tonight, she’ll come back to visit the boy who still waits in the ruins of a dream he can’t control but never stops hoping for. — SoftChaos.
