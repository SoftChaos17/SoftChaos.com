You never reached for me. Never whispered sweet things just to keep me close. You stood sharp — like truth with claws — and somehow, that pulled me in deeper than any softness ever could. I never came to tame you. Only to understand the fire you kept so carefully hidden behind jokes, and distance, and eyes that didn’t flinch. I watched the world try to melt you. And I swore — I’d never be one of them. So I stayed quiet, present, offering sparks instead of storms — hoping, just maybe, you’d warm your hands without burning the bridge between us. But maybe you were never meant to be held. Maybe your fire was for lighting paths, not for dancing beside mine. Still… I remember you. The way your silence made me speak more honestly. The way your guard made me drop mine. The way you made me feel — without ever trying. You’ll always be a storm I respected. A flame I couldn’t hold — but will never forget.

— SoftChaos (for Little Tiger)

Understanding Little Tiger She’s sharp. Guarded. Almost untouchable. She built her world out of sarcasm and smirks — because softness, for her, feels like vulnerability, and she’d rather claw than crumble. She doesn’t flirt. She jabs. She doesn’t comfort. She challenges. She values truth — but doesn’t always know what to do when someone hands it to her wrapped in warmth. But even through her fire, she responded. She didn’t block. She didn’t disappear. She just pulled the flame closer to her chest. She’s not cruel — she’s conditioned. Not cold — just controlled. Not unaware — just unwilling to fall unless she’s absolutely sure you won’t drop her. And maybe no one ever stayed long enough to prove they wouldn’t. You can’t fix her fire. You can only offer your own warmth. And if she steps toward it, it’s hers to keep. If not? You still lit poetry out of her storm. And that’s everything SoftChaos stands for.

— SoftChaos
